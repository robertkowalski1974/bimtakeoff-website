Commercial Project Types

Understanding Commercial Development Complexity

Small-to-Medium Office

Size: 1,000-5,000 sqm
Type: Low-to-mid rise
Complexity: Medium
Timeline: 3-5 days
Typical Cost: £2,200-3,200/sqm

Characteristics: - 3-6 storey construction - Standard office specification - Natural ventilation or FCUs - Surface or basement parking - Standard finishes

Grade A Office Building

Size: 5,000-20,000 sqm
Type: Mid-to-high rise
Complexity: High
Timeline: 6-8 days
Typical Cost: £2,800-4,200/sqm

Characteristics: - RC frame, 6-15 storeys - Grade A specification - VRV/VRF air conditioning - Raised floors, suspended ceilings - Premium façade systems - Multi-level parking

Mixed-Use Development

Size: 10,000-50,000+ sqm
Type: Complex high-rise
Complexity: Very High
Timeline: 8-10 days
Typical Cost: £2,500-4,500/sqm

Characteristics: - Multiple use types - Office, retail, residential - Complex interfaces - Shared services/cores - Extensive public realm - Phased delivery

Key Cost Drivers in Commercial Projects

Typical Grade A Office Breakdown (per sqm)

Structure & Envelope (40-45%)

  • Foundations & substructure: £280-420/sqm
  • RC frame & floors: £380-580/sqm
  • Façade & glazing: £350-550/sqm
  • Roofing: £80-140/sqm
  • Subtotal: £1,090-1,690/sqm

Internal Fit-Out (20-25%)

  • Raised access floors: £60-100/sqm
  • Suspended ceilings: £80-120/sqm
  • Internal partitions: £50-90/sqm
  • Finishes & decorations: £70-110/sqm
  • Subtotal: £260-420/sqm

MEP Services (25-30%)

  • VRV/VRF air conditioning: £200-320/sqm
  • Electrical & lighting: £120-180/sqm
  • IT & communications: £60-100/sqm
  • Plumbing & drainage: £50-80/sqm
  • Fire protection & alarms: £60-100/sqm
  • Subtotal: £490-780/sqm

Other Elements (10-15%)

  • Lifts & escalators: £80-150/sqm
  • External works: £100-180/sqm
  • Parking (allowance): £80-140/sqm
  • Preliminaries (10-12%): £280-420/sqm
  • Contingency (5-8%): £150-280/sqm
  • Subtotal: £690-1,170/sqm

Total Typical Range: £2,530-4,060/sqm

(Shell & core + CAT A fit-out, excluding land and fees)

NOTE: Actual costs vary significantly based on:

  • Location and ground conditions
  • Building height and floor-to-floor heights
  • Specification level (Grade A vs Grade B)
  • MEP system complexity and efficiency
  • Façade design and thermal performance
  • BREEAM/WELL/EPC rating targets

Our Commercial Estimation Process

Initial Review & Setup

Day 1 (4-6 hours)

What We Do: - Review BIM model (IFC/Revit) or 2D drawings - Verify building dimensions and floor areas - Identify specification level requirements - Assess ESG/sustainability targets - Set up cost breakdown structure

Client Provides: - Architectural & structural drawings/models - MEP design information - Specification documents - Landlord/tenant fit-out split - Any sustainability targets

Output: Project plan and timeline confirmation

Automated Quantity Extraction

Days 1-4

What We Do: - Extract all structural elements - Quantify façade and glazing - Measure internal fit-out areas - Calculate MEP requirements - Count sanitary fixtures, lifts, etc.

Technology: - BIM 5D extraction tools - Floor area analysis - Cross-checking with specifications

Output: Complete quantity take-off (BOQ)

Cost Application & Analysis

Days 4-7

What We Do: - Apply current market rates - Factor in location adjustments - Calculate MEP systems costs - Include fit-out specifications - Add preliminaries (10-12%) - Apply contingency (5-8%) - Run alternative scenarios if requested

Database Sources: - BCIS (UK) / Rawlinsons (Australia) - Live specialist quotes - Recent commercial benchmarks

Output: Detailed cost estimate with breakdown

Quality Review & Reporting

Days 7-9

What We Do: - Technical peer review - Check against market benchmarks - Verify yield implications - Prepare visual cost breakdown - Create executive summary - Schedule presentation/meeting

Deliverables: - Executive summary (2-3 pages) - Cost breakdown by element - Shell vs fit-out separation - BOQ with quantities and rates - Cost per sqm analysis - Alternative scenarios (if requested)

Format: PDF + Excel workbooks

Presentation & Handover

Day 9-10

What We Do: - Present findings to development team - Explain key cost drivers - Review value engineering options - Discuss yield optimization - Answer questions - Provide ongoing support

Meeting Options: - In-person (UK only) - Video conference (all locations) - Investment committee presentations

Support: 30 days post-delivery included

Typical Total Duration: 7-10 working days from BIM model receipt to final report
Express Service: Available for urgent projects (4-5 days) at 25% premium

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What’s the largest commercial project you’ve estimated?

A: We’ve worked on office buildings up to 50,000 sqm and mixed-use developments exceeding 100,000 sqm total area. Our 5D BIM workflow scales effectively for major commercial projects including multi-tower developments.

Q: Can you separate shell & core from CAT A fit-out costs?

A: Absolutely. We routinely provide cost breakdowns showing base build (shell & core), landlord fit-out (CAT A), and tenant fit-out (CAT B) separately. This is essential for investment analysis and lease negotiations.

Q: How quickly can you provide a feasibility estimate for investment decisions?

A: For early-stage feasibility estimates based on concept designs, we can provide cost ranges within 2-3 days using benchmarks and parametric estimating. This gives investors the confidence to proceed to detailed design.

Q: What if the tenant wants different specifications after your estimate?

A: Because we work with 5D BIM, we can quickly recalculate costs for specification changes within hours to a day. This is particularly valuable during pre-let negotiations when tenants request variations.

Q: Do you include BREEAM/WELL certification costs?

A: Yes, we include both the construction cost premium for achieving target ratings (e.g., BREEAM Excellent) and the professional fees for assessment and certification. We can model different rating scenarios to help optimize investment returns.

Q: Can you provide cost breakdowns by floor or phase?

A: Yes, we regularly prepare cost breakdowns by floor, wing, or construction phase. This is particularly useful for phased developments or buildings with varying specifications across different floors.

Q: How do you handle complex MEP systems like VRV/VRF?

A: We have extensive experience costing advanced MEP systems including VRV/VRF air conditioning, displacement ventilation, and sophisticated BMS controls. Our estimates reflect the complexity of modern Grade A office specifications.

Q: What’s your fee structure for commercial projects?

A: Fees vary based on project size, complexity, and timeline. As a rough guide: - Small office (1,000-5,000 sqm): £3,000-6,000 - Medium office (5,000-15,000 sqm): £6,000-12,000 - Large office (15,000-30,000 sqm): £12,000-20,000 - Major/mixed-use (30,000+ sqm): £20,000-40,000

Express service (4-5 days) carries a 25% premium. Contact us for a specific quote.
