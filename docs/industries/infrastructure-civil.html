Infrastructure Project Types

Understanding Civil Engineering Project Complexity

Local Roads & Access

Length: 500m - 2km
Type: Single carriageway
Complexity: Medium
Timeline: 7-8 days
Typical Cost: £800-1,500 per linear meter

Characteristics: - Estate roads & access ways - Standard construction depths - Basic drainage systems - Street lighting & furniture - Limited earthworks - Standard utilities diversions

Major Roads & Junctions

Length: 2-10km
Type: Dual carriageway/major junctions
Complexity: High
Timeline: 9-11 days
Typical Cost: £1,500-3,500 per linear meter

Characteristics: - Dual carriageway sections - Complex junction works - Significant earthworks - Major drainage networks - Bridges/structures (up to 50m span) - Extensive utilities coordination - Traffic management complexity

Major Infrastructure

Length: 10km+ or complex structures
Type: Highways/major bridges
Complexity: Very High
Timeline: 11-12 days
Typical Cost: £3,000-8,000+ per linear meter

Characteristics: - Motorway/highway construction - Major bridge structures (50m+ span) - Tunnels & underpasses - Complex geotechnical works - Environmental mitigations - Major utility diversions - Extensive traffic management - Multi-phase delivery

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why do infrastructure projects have higher uncertainty than buildings?

A: Infrastructure projects face inherent uncertainty from ground conditions (revealed only during construction), utility clashes (survey limitations), linear project variability (changing conditions over distance), and environmental constraints (protected species, contamination). We address this through ±5-8% accuracy ranges and explicit risk allowances for these factors.

Q: Can you optimize earthworks for cut/fill balance?

A: Yes, we use mass haul analysis to optimize cut/fill balance, minimizing import/export costs and environmental impact. Our BIM workflows calculate earthworks volumes along the entire route and model different vertical alignment scenarios to find the optimal balance. We can save projects 10-20% on earthworks costs through optimization.

Q: How do you handle utility diversion costs?

A: We systematically assess utility diversions based on survey data, identifying clashes and protection requirements. Our estimates include diversion works, protective measures, temporary diversions, and utility company charges. We factor in risk allowances for unexpected utilities as surveys are rarely 100% comprehensive.

Q: Can you cost bridge structures and major civil works?

A: Absolutely. We have extensive experience costing bridge structures (RC and steel), retaining walls, culverts, and underpasses. For complex structures over 50m span, we work with specialist structural engineers to ensure accuracy. Our estimates include foundations, substructures, superstructures, parapets, and finishes.

Q: How do you account for ground condition risks?

A: We review geotechnical reports to understand ground risks and include appropriate contingencies (typically 10-15% for ground-related risks). We separately identify rock excavation allowances, contamination treatment, ground improvement, and dewatering requirements based on site investigation findings. We can model different ground scenarios if uncertainty is high.

Q: Can you model phased construction to maintain traffic flow?

A: Yes, we regularly prepare phased delivery estimates that maintain operational access. We model traffic management costs, night/weekend working premiums, diversion routes, and the productivity impact of working in constrained conditions. This is critical for highway improvement projects and works in urban areas.

Q: How quickly can you assess alternative route alignments?

A: For route optioneering, we can typically compare 2-3 alignment alternatives within 5-7 days. Our BIM workflows allow rapid calculation of earthworks volumes, pavement quantities, and structure requirements for each option. This is valuable during early design stages when route selection is still being finalized.

Q: What’s your fee structure for infrastructure projects?

A: Fees vary based on project type, length/scale, and complexity. As a guide: - Local roads (500m-2km): £12,000-20,000 - Major roads (2-10km): £25,000-45,000 - Major infrastructure (10km+ or complex): £45,000-80,000+ - Bridge structures: £8,000-25,000 per structure (depending on span/complexity)
