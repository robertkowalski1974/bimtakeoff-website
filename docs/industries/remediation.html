Building Safety Regulator (BSR) Expertise

Navigating the New Building Safety Regime

The Building Safety Act 2022 introduced the Building Safety Regulator (BSR) under the Health and Safety Executive (HSE), fundamentally changing how Higher Risk Buildings (HRB) are designed, constructed, and maintained. We provide specialized cost estimation services aligned with BSR requirements.

Higher Risk Buildings (HRB) Definition

Buildings Requiring BSR Registration: - 18 metres or more in height, OR - 7 storeys or more, AND - Contains at least 2 residential units

Our Cost Support: - Registration compliance costs - Mandatory occurrence reporting systems - Resident engagement requirements - Safety case preparation budgets

Gateway Process Costing

Gateway 2 (Planning Stage): - Fire statement preparation - Building Safety Act compliance review - BSR submission documentation

Gateway 3 (Pre-Construction): - Detailed design compliance - Fire and structural strategies - Change control procedures - Principal Designer costs

Building Safety Case Support

Safety Case Development Costs: - Risk assessments & evaluations - Building management structure - Safety management systems - Documentation & compliance records - Ongoing monitoring requirements - Mandatory occurrence reporting

BSR Submission Cost Estimation

We provide detailed cost breakdowns for BSR-related activities:

Registration & Compliance (Typical Costs): - BSR registration application: £2,000-4,000 - Building Safety Case preparation: £15,000-35,000 - Resident engagement strategy: £5,000-12,000 - Mandatory occurrence reporting system: £3,000-8,000 - Annual compliance review: £8,000-15,000

Professional Team Requirements: - Principal Designer appointment - Principal Contractor obligations - Building Safety Manager (in occupation) - Accountable Person duties - Competence framework compliance - Change control management

Remediation-Specific BSR Requirements

For existing HRBs requiring remediation, BSR compliance adds specific cost implications:

Enhanced Surveys & Assessments - Type 4 Fire Risk Assessment - Intrusive building investigations - Structural integrity reviews - External wall system (EWS1) assessments - Golden thread data creation

Compliance Documentation - Safety case updates during works - Change control procedures - Contractor competence verification - Mandatory occurrence reporting - Resident notification protocols

Post-Remediation Requirements - Updated building safety information - Revised safety case submission - As-built BIM models (ISO 19650) - Resident information updates - BSR notification of completion

Important BSR Deadline Context

All existing HRBs must be registered with BSR. Accountable Persons face significant penalties for non-compliance. Our cost estimates help building owners budget for mandatory compliance upgrades alongside remediation works.

Common Remediation Project Types

Understanding Remediation Complexity

Cladding Remediation

Building Height: Typically 18m+
Complexity: High
Timeline: 5-8 days
Typical Cost: £3,000-8,000 per unit

Scope Includes: - ACM/HPL cladding removal - Compliant system installation - Fire barriers & cavity barriers - External wall insulation (if required) - Balcony works & fire stopping - Scaffolding & protection - Building survey & testing

Fire Safety Upgrades

Building Type: Multi-occupancy
Complexity: Medium-High
Timeline: 4-6 days
Typical Cost: £1,500-4,000 per unit

Scope Includes: - Compartmentation upgrades - Fire door replacements - Smoke ventilation (AOV) - Fire alarm systems - Emergency lighting - Sprinkler systems (if required) - Wayfinding & signage

Structural Repairs

Building Age: Variable
Complexity: High
Timeline: 5-7 days
Typical Cost: £2,000-6,000 per unit

Scope Includes: - Concrete repairs (spalling) - Structural strengthening - Balcony reconstruction - Movement joint repairs - Waterproofing systems - Underpinning (if required) - Temporary propping

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can you estimate remediation costs without intrusive surveys?

A: We can provide indicative budgets based on visual surveys and typical defect patterns, but we strongly recommend intrusive investigation before finalizing estimates. We typically include 20-25% contingency for non-intrusive estimates vs 10-15% for fully surveyed buildings.

Q: How do you handle unknown defects discovered during works?

A: Our estimates include risk-based contingencies (typically 15-25%) calculated from intrusive survey findings and industry benchmarks. We also provide a risk register identifying potential additional costs with probability assessments.

Q: Can you support Building Safety Fund (BSF) applications?

A: Yes, we regularly prepare cost documentation for BSF and other grant applications. We provide the detailed breakdown formats required by funding bodies and can support the technical review process.

Q: What about phasing to keep residents in occupation?

A: We specialize in occupied building remediation. Our estimates include phasing strategies, protection costs, temporary works, resident liaison, and the cost premium for working around occupants (typically 15-20% additional).

Q: How accurate are remediation estimates given the unknowns?

A: With proper intrusive surveys, we achieve ±5-8% accuracy on remediation projects (vs our standard ±5% for new build). The additional variance accounts for unexpected defects. We provide clear contingency breakdowns for transparency.

Q: Can you estimate alternative cladding systems for comparison?

A: Absolutely. We regularly compare different compliant cladding systems (brick slip, render, metal panels, terracotta) with full cost implications including performance characteristics, maintenance, and longevity considerations.

Q: What about asbestos or other hazardous materials?

A: We include allowances for asbestos surveys and removal when building age suggests likely presence. We work with specialist asbestos surveyors to refine removal costs and include proper disposal, air monitoring, and notification costs.

Q: What’s your fee structure for remediation projects?

A: Fees vary based on building size, defect complexity, and urgency. As a rough guide: - Small building (20-50 units): £3,500-6,000 - Medium building (50-100 units): £6,000-10,000 - Large building (100-200 units): £10,000-16,000 - Complex multi-block: £16,000-30,000

Express service (3-4 days) carries a 25% premium. Contact us for a specific quote.
