Housing Association Project Types

Understanding Social Housing Complexity

New Build Social Housing

Units: 50-200 dwellings
Type: Affordable housing
Complexity: Medium
Timeline: 5-8 days
Typical Cost: £1,600-2,200/sqm

Characteristics: - 2-3 storey construction - Standard specifications - Homes England grant funded - Mixed tenure (social rent, shared ownership) - Basic amenities - Cost-per-unit focus

Stock Refurbishment

Units: 100-500+ properties
Type: Planned maintenance
Complexity: Medium-High
Timeline: 6-9 days
Typical Cost: £8,000-25,000/unit

Characteristics: - Kitchen & bathroom replacement - Heating system upgrades - Window & door replacement - External wall insulation - Roof replacement - Occupied properties - Decent Homes compliance

Key Cost Drivers in Housing Association Projects

Typical Refurbishment Programme Breakdown (per unit)

Internal Upgrades (40-50%)

  • Kitchen replacement: £3,000-5,000/unit
  • Bathroom replacement: £2,500-4,000/unit
  • Heating system: £2,000-3,500/unit
  • Electrical rewiring: £1,500-2,500/unit
  • Subtotal: £9,000-15,000/unit

External Works (25-35%)

  • External wall insulation: £4,000-7,000/unit
  • Window replacement: £2,000-3,500/unit
  • Roof works: £1,500-3,000/unit
  • External doors: £800-1,200/unit
  • Subtotal: £8,300-14,700/unit

Building Services (10-15%)

  • Communal heating upgrades: £1,000-2,000/unit
  • Electrical distribution: £800-1,500/unit
  • Ventilation systems: £500-1,000/unit
  • Fire alarm upgrades: £400-800/unit
  • Subtotal: £2,700-5,300/unit

Other Costs (10-15%)

  • Asbestos surveys/removal: £500-1,200/unit
  • Decanting costs: £800-1,500/unit
  • Professional fees (8-10%): £1,600-3,000/unit
  • Contingency (5-8%): £1,000-2,000/unit
  • Subtotal: £3,900-7,700/unit

Total Typical Range: £23,900-42,700 per unit

(Comprehensive Decent Homes refurbishment programme)

NOTE: Actual costs vary significantly based on:

  • Property age and condition
  • Building type (low-rise vs high-rise)
  • Occupied vs vacant possession
  • Scope of works (full vs partial upgrade)
  • EPC improvement targets (C, B, or A rating)
  • Local labour and material rates

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can you help with grant funding applications?

A: Yes, we regularly prepare detailed cost documentation for grant funding applications including Social Housing Decarbonisation Fund, Affordable Homes Programme, and local authority grants. We provide the cost breakdowns and specifications required by funding bodies.

Q: How do you handle properties with different conditions and sizes?

A: We create property archetypes based on building type, size, and condition, then calculate costs for each archetype. This provides accurate per-unit costs while maintaining detailed breakdowns for the overall programme.

Q: Can you provide materials for resident consultation?

A: Yes, we prepare resident-friendly consultation materials including visual presentations, cost breakdown summaries, alternative option comparisons, and Q&A documentation suitable for member assemblies and individual consultations.

Q: What about estimating for occupied properties?

A: We specialize in occupied building works. Our estimates include phasing strategies, protection measures, decanting costs (where necessary), resident liaison time, and the cost premium for working around occupants (typically 10-15% additional).

Q: How accurate are estimates for Decent Homes compliance?

A: With proper stock condition surveys, we achieve ±5-8% accuracy on Decent Homes refurbishment programmes. We include appropriate contingencies (5-10%) for properties where condition surveys reveal uncertainties.

Q: How do you approach value engineering for tight budgets?

A: We identify value engineering opportunities without compromising quality or compliance. This includes optimizing specifications, exploring alternative materials, value-analyzing phasing options, and maximizing volume discounts through strategic procurement.

Q: What’s your fee structure for housing association projects?

A: Fees vary based on programme size and complexity. As a rough guide: - Small programme (50-100 units): £3,000-6,000 - Medium programme (100-300 units): £6,000-12,000 - Large programme (300-500 units): £12,000-20,000

Express service (4-5 days) carries a 25% premium. Contact us for a specific quote.
