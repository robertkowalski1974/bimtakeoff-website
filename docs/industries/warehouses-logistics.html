Warehouse Project Complexity Factors

Understanding Your Project Scale

Standard Single-Unit Warehouse

Size: 5,000 - 20,000 sqm
Height: 10-12m clear
Complexity: Low-Medium
Timeline: 3-4 days
Typical Cost: £600-900/sqm

Characteristics: - Single tenant - Standard steel frame - Basic cladding & roofing - 5-10 loading docks - Minimal office space

Multi-Unit Distribution Center

Size: 20,000 - 50,000 sqm
Height: 12-15m clear
Complexity: Medium-High
Timeline: 5-6 days
Typical Cost: £700-1,100/sqm

Characteristics: - 2-4 tenant spaces - Automated racking systems - Enhanced fire protection - 15-25 loading docks - Mezzanine office areas

Large-Scale Fulfillment Hub

Size: 50,000 - 100,000+ sqm
Height: 15m+ clear
Complexity: High
Timeline: 6-7 days
Typical Cost: £800-1,500/sqm

Characteristics: - Single mega-tenant - Automation infrastructure - Complex MEP systems - 50+ loading docks - Extensive office/amenity space

Key Cost Drivers in Warehouse Projects

Typical Cost Breakdown (per sqm)

Structural Elements (40-45%)

  • Steel frame: £180-250/sqm
  • Concrete floor slab: £80-120/sqm
  • Foundations: £60-100/sqm
  • Subtotal: £320-470/sqm

Envelope (20-25%)

  • Wall cladding: £60-90/sqm
  • Roof covering: £50-80/sqm
  • Loading dock equipment: £30-50/sqm
  • Subtotal: £140-220/sqm

MEP Services (15-20%)

  • Electrical distribution: £40-60/sqm
  • Lighting: £25-40/sqm
  • Heating/ventilation: £30-50/sqm
  • Fire protection: £20-35/sqm
  • Subtotal: £115-185/sqm

Other Elements (15-20%)

  • External works: £50-80/sqm
  • Preliminaries (12-15%): £85-150/sqm
  • Contingency (5-10%): £40-80/sqm
  • Subtotal: £175-310/sqm

Total Typical Range: £750-1,185/sqm

(Excluding land, fees, and client fit-out)

NOTE: Actual costs vary significantly based on:

  • Location and ground conditions
  • Height and clear span requirements
  • Automation and racking systems
  • Fire protection standards
  • Tenant specification level

Our Warehouse Estimation Process

Initial Review & Setup

Day 1 (4-6 hours)

What We Do: - Review BIM model (IFC/Revit) or 2D drawings - Verify structural grid and bay sizes - Confirm clear height requirements - Identify tenant-specific requirements - Set up repetitive element libraries

Client Provides: - Architectural & structural drawings/models - Specification documents - Site information - Any tenant fit-out requirements

Output: Project plan and timeline confirmation

Automated Quantity Extraction

Days 1-2

What We Do: - Extract all structural elements automatically - Quantify cladding and roofing systems - Calculate foundation volumes - Count loading docks and doors - Measure floor areas and wall lengths

Technology: - BIM 5D extraction tools - Repetitive element automation - Cross-checking with 2D documents

Output: Complete quantity take-off (BOQ)

Cost Application & Analysis

Days 2-4

What We Do: - Apply current market rates - Factor in project location - Include logistics considerations - Calculate preliminaries (12-15%) - Add appropriate contingency (5-10%) - Run alternative scenarios if requested

Database Sources: - BCIS (UK) / Rawlinsons (Australia) - Live supplier quotes - Recent project benchmarks

Output: Detailed cost estimate with breakdown

Quality Review & Reporting

Days 4-5

What We Do: - Technical peer review - Check against benchmarks - Verify calculations - Prepare visual cost breakdown - Create executive summary - Schedule presentation/meeting

Deliverables: - Executive summary (2-3 pages) - Detailed cost breakdown by element - BOQ with quantities and rates - Cost per sqm analysis - Alternative scenarios (if requested)

Format: PDF + Excel workbooks

Presentation & Handover

Day 5-7

What We Do: - Present findings to project team - Explain key cost drivers - Review any value engineering options - Discuss any risks or assumptions - Answer questions - Provide ongoing support

Meeting Options: - In-person (UK only) - Video conference (all locations) - Written Q&A via email

Support: 30 days post-delivery included

Typical Total Duration: 5-7 working days from BIM model receipt to final report
Express Service: Available for urgent projects (3-4 days) at 25% premium

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What’s the largest warehouse project you’ve estimated?

A: We’ve worked on distribution centers up to 150,000 sqm single-tenant facilities and multi-unit parks totaling 300,000+ sqm. No project is too large for our 5D BIM workflow.

Q: Can you handle pre-engineered building systems (Butler, Kirby, etc.)?

A: Absolutely. We have extensive experience with all major pre-engineered building systems including Butler, Kirby, Astron, Rubb, and Frisomat. Our cost databases include standard configurations for these systems.

Q: How quickly can you provide a budget estimate for planning purposes?

A: For a planning-stage budget estimate based on concept drawings, we can typically provide a cost range within 1-2 days. This uses benchmarks and parametric estimating rather than detailed take-offs.

Q: What if the client changes the clear height requirement after your estimate?

A: Because we work with 5D BIM, we can quickly re-calculate the cost impact of height changes, typically within a few hours. This includes structural implications, cladding adjustments, and MEP modifications.

Q: Do you include automated material handling systems in your estimates?

A: We can include allowances for automated storage and retrieval systems (ASRS), conveyor systems, and sortation equipment. For complex automation, we typically work with specialist suppliers to get accurate budgets.

Q: Can you provide cost breakdowns by zone for phased construction?

A: Yes, we regularly prepare cost breakdowns by construction phase or tenant zone. This is particularly useful for speculative development where different units may be built or fitted out at different times.

Q: How do you handle site-specific conditions like poor ground?

A: We rely on the client’s ground investigation reports to assess foundation requirements. For poor ground conditions, we include appropriate foundation solutions (piling, ground improvement, etc.) based on the geotechnical recommendations.

Q: What’s your fee structure for warehouse projects?

A: Fees vary based on project size, complexity, and timeline. As a rough guide: - Small warehouse (5,000-10,000 sqm): £3,000-5,000 - Medium warehouse (10,000-30,000 sqm): £5,000-8,000 - Large warehouse (30,000-50,000 sqm): £8,000-12,000 - Mega facility (50,000+ sqm): £12,000-20,000

Express service (3-4 days) carries a 25% premium. Contact us for a specific quote.
