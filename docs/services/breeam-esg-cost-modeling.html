Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What’s the typical cost premium for BREEAM Excellent vs Good?

A: Based on our experience, BREEAM Excellent adds 3-5% to construction costs vs BREEAM Good, while Outstanding adds 6-8%. However, this varies significantly by building type, scale, and baseline specification. Our modeling provides project-specific figures with credit-by-credit breakdown, helping you target the most cost-effective rating level.

Q: How do you calculate embodied carbon costs?

A: We use comprehensive carbon databases (ICE, EPDs) to calculate kgCO2e per material/element. This is converted to cost using current and projected carbon pricing (£50-£150/tonne scenarios). We assess both upfront embodied carbon (materials, construction) and whole-life carbon (maintenance, replacement, end-of-life) to provide complete picture.

Q: Can you model ROI for net zero investments?

A: Yes, our 30-year life cycle cost models include: energy cost savings, carbon taxation avoidance, maintenance differentials, enhanced asset value, regulatory compliance costs, and grant/incentive capture. We provide NPV, IRR, and payback period analysis suitable for board-level decision making.

Q: Do you work with projects targeting multiple certifications?

A: Absolutely. Many projects pursue BREEAM + WELL, or LEED + Net Zero Carbon. We identify credit overlaps to minimize cost duplication, prioritize certifications based on market value, and optimize the overall sustainability strategy for maximum certification achievement at lowest cost.

Q: How do you handle uncertainty in future carbon pricing?

A: We model multiple scenarios (conservative £50/tonne, medium £100/tonne, aggressive £150/tonne) based on UK ETS trends and policy forecasts. This provides risk range rather than single-point estimate, allowing informed decision-making under uncertainty. We update scenarios as policy evolves.

Q: Can you cost biodiversity net gain requirements?

A: Yes, we cost on-site ecological enhancements, off-site habitat creation, biodiversity offsetting payments, ongoing management commitments, and monitoring requirements. We use Defra biodiversity metrics and habitat banking rates to provide accurate 30-year cost projections.

Q: What if we need to value engineer without losing certification?

A: Our value engineering approach prioritizes cost reductions that don’t impact certification. We re-target credits (e.g., drop expensive credits, pursue cheaper ones), optimize system specifications, phase implementation, and leverage innovation credits. Our goal: achieve certification at lowest cost.

Q: How do you price ESG reporting compliance?

A: ESG reporting costs include: data collection systems, monitoring equipment, third-party verification, annual reporting preparation, and certification maintenance. We model these as operational costs (£5K-£50K annually depending on framework) and include in whole-life cost analysis.
