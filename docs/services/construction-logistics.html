Our Proven 5-Step Logistics Planning Process

Step 1: Site Analysis & Constraints

Duration: 3-5 days

What We Do:

  • Site visit and survey (topography, access, boundaries)
  • Review site constraints (neighbours, highways, utilities)
  • Identify permit and approval requirements
  • Assess existing structures and obstructions
  • Understand client operational constraints
  • Review programme and key milestones

What You Receive: Site constraints report with opportunities/risks

Step 2: Concept Logistics Planning

Duration: 5-10 days

What We Do:

  • Develop site layout options (2-3 alternatives)
  • Preliminary crane/hoist positioning
  • Identify temporary works requirements
  • Plan major deliveries and access routes
  • Assess welfare and facility needs
  • Present options with pros/cons

What You Receive: Concept logistics plans for review

Step 3: Detailed Design & 3D Modeling

Duration: 10-15 days

What We Do:

  • Develop selected option into detailed design
  • Create 3D site model with temporary works
  • Perform crane coverage and over-sailing analysis
  • Design delivery routes and turning circles
  • Coordinate with subcontractors and suppliers
  • Check compliance with regulations

What You Receive: Detailed 2D/3D logistics plans

Step 4: 4D Simulation & Optimization

Duration: 5-10 days

What We Do:

  • Link site model to construction programme
  • Create 4D construction sequence simulation
  • Identify spatial and temporal conflicts
  • Optimize phasing and work fronts
  • Generate animations for stakeholder communication
  • Refine programme based on 4D insights

What You Receive: 4D animations and optimized programme

Step 5: Implementation Support

Duration: Ongoing (as needed)

What We Do:

  • Prepare site setup drawings for contractor
  • Support pre-construction planning meetings
  • Assist with temporary works submissions
  • Update plans for design/programme changes
  • Provide on-site support during mobilization

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: When should we engage logistics planning on a project?

A: Ideally at RIBA Stage 3 (Spatial Coordination) or earlier for complex sites. This allows logistics constraints to inform design decisions. Absolute latest: 8-12 weeks before site mobilization. Urban projects with crane permits require earlier engagement (16-20 weeks before start) to allow for authority approvals.

Q: Can you help us win tenders by showing better logistics solutions than competitors?

A: Absolutely! High-quality logistics planning in tender submissions demonstrates competence and de-risks the project for clients. We’ve helped contractors win multiple projects by presenting superior site planning, phasing strategies, and 4D animations that competitors couldn’t match. Typical investment: £8K-15K, often the margin between winning and losing £10M+ contracts.

Q: Do you handle temporary works design or just coordination?

A: We coordinate temporary works requirements and prepare schedules, but detailed structural design (scaffolding, falsework, propping) must be by certified Temporary Works Design companies. We work closely with TWC (Temporary Works Coordinator) and TWS (Temporary Works Supervisor) to ensure integrated planning. We can recommend specialist TW design firms if needed.

Q: Can you optimize our existing site layout to improve efficiency?

A: Yes! Mid-project optimization is common. We analyze current layouts, identify bottlenecks and inefficiencies, and propose improvements. Typical savings: 10-20% improvement in productivity through better material placement, optimized routes, and reduced double-handling. Most beneficial when implemented during natural break points (e.g., topping out, phase transitions).

Q: What if we don’t have a BIM model yet?

A: We can work from 2D drawings to create 3D site models for logistics planning. For early-stage projects, even sketch designs are sufficient for concept logistics. The earlier we’re engaged, the more value we add - but we can deliver useful planning at any stage, even with limited design information.

Q: How accurate are your crane coverage analyses?

A: Very accurate when based on manufacturer specifications. We use actual crane data from Liebherr, Potain, Wolff, etc. Analysis includes wind speed deratings, load charts, and boom configurations. We identify 100% of coverage gaps and over-sailing conflicts in 3D before crane selection. Typical outcome: optimal crane positioning saving £50K-200K vs “guessed” placement.

Q: Can you create 4D animations for client presentations?

A: Yes! 4D animations are excellent stakeholder communication tools. We create professional animations showing construction sequences, phasing, and logistics operations over time. These are invaluable for: client confidence, planning approvals, neighbor engagement, and internal team coordination. Typical cost: £5K-15K for 2-5 minute professional animation depending on complexity.

Q: Do you provide on-site support during construction?

A: Yes, we offer weekly or monthly site visits for logistics coordination support. Services include: reviewing actual vs planned layouts, updating plans for design changes, optimizing phasing based on progress, coordinating major lifts and deliveries, and attending planning meetings. Typical cost: £1,500-3,000 per visit or monthly retainer £4K-8K.

Q: What’s the typical ROI on logistics planning?

A: Clients typically save 5-15x the planning cost through: avoided crane hire errors (£50K-200K), optimized programme (2-6 weeks saved = £60K-180K prelims), reduced rework and material damage (£20K-100K), and prevented regulatory delays (invaluable). On a £20M project, £25K logistics planning investment typically returns £200K-500K in savings and risk mitigation.
